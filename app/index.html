Optimization continues to create new challenges, both with respect to modeling and algorithms. The successful conferences at Princeton University in 2016 and Rice University in 2014 highlighted the challenges that we will continue to explore at this conference.

The theme of "mountains of optimization" is designed to emphasize, in our mountain setting, the myriad of topics, both theoretical and applied, that confront optimizers today.

Directions from DenverAirport Rail (A Line) to UCD

At a cost of $9 each way, travelers can use the rail service, known as the A Line, to get from the airport to Denver Union Station and vice versa. It takes around an hour and half to travel from your gate to campus. Trains leave every 15 minutes during most parts of the day, daily train schedules can be found here.

When leaving the airport look for signs to “Transit Center”. It’s going to be on the same side of the airport as the Westin Hotel, Plaza. Purchase your train ticket at the ticket vending machines near the train platform or at the RTD Customer Care counter located in the middle of Denver Airport Station.

Ticket vending machines are not available on the train platform – please purchase your fare before boarding the train. You may be asked to show your ticket so keep it accessible. The train platform (in the picture to the right) only has one train line so feel free to jump on the next train that is available.

It will take about 40 minutes to travel from the Airport station to Union station. Once you arrive at Union Station, you can take a free bus down 16th street or stretch your legs with a 15 minute walk to 1201 Larimer Street.

AMPL Optimization develops and supports a powerful yet natural modeling language and system for creating and managing large, complex optimization problems. AMPL is notable for supporting a broad range of linear and nonlinear problem formulations and a large selection of popular large-scale solvers. New features include an integrated editor and command processor for development, and programming interfaces for integration into applications.
